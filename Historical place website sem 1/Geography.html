Geography of Hampi.

cuisines of state

The town of Hampi is located in the Southern state of Karnataka, where the food is fresher and lighter than the cuisines to the north. Traditional breakfasts in this part of India largely consist of idly, vada, and sambar, while lunch and dinner have curries in a thali. Some of the popular dishes of hampi are badanekayi which is stuffed brinjal, anna, saaru (a soup), kosambari, kempu chutney, mosaru bajji, happala, benne tuppa. These restaurants specialise in serving assorted non-vegetarian thalis to their customers. The tourists who visit there must try this famous cuisine of hampi once.

As part of Karnataka, Hampi is known for its South Indian cuisine. You'll find traditional South Indian dishes like dosa (rice crepes), idli (steamed rice cakes), vada (fried lentil dumplings), and uttapam (thick rice pancakes) served in many local restaurants and street food stalls. These dishes are typically accompanied by coconut chutney, sambar (a spicy lentil soup), and various other condiments.


Karnataka has its own unique culinary traditions, and you can try local dishes like Bisi Bele Bath (a spicy rice dish), Ragi Mudde (finger millet balls), and Neer Dosa (thin rice crepes). These dishes often use a blend of spices and ingredients specific to the region. Vegetarian food is prevalent in Hampi and the surrounding regions.Many local restaurants and eateries serve vegetarian dishes due to cultural and religious preferences.

UNESCO World Heritage Site: In 1986, Hampi was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in recognition of its historical and cultural significance. The site attracts historians, archaeologists, architecture enthusiasts, and tourists from around the world.


Present-day Hampi: While the city itself is in ruins, the village of Hampi continues to be inhabited and serves as a gateway for tourists exploring the archaeological site. It offers various accommodations, restaurants, and services for visitors.


Hampi is not only a testament to India's glorious past but also a place of cultural, architectural, and historical importance that provides a glimpse into the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire.


The empire reached its zenith under the rule of Emperor Krishnadevaraya in the 16th century. During this period, Hampi became a center of art, culture, and trade, and it flourished with magnificent temples, palaces, and infrastructure. After the invasion, Hampi was largely abandoned and left in ruins. The once-thriving city fell into obscurity.

Today, Hampi stands as a remarkable archaeological and historical site, offering a glimpse into the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire and its architectural, cultural, and religious achievements.
