Badami: Located about 2 hours from Hampi, Badami is renowned for its rock-cut cave temples, intricate sculptures, and ancient architecture.


Aihole: Aihole, often referred to as the "Cradle of Indian Architecture," is known for its exquisite ancient temples. The Badami Cave Temples, Agastya Lake, and Badami Fort are among the top attractions here. It's about 2.5 hours from Hampi and offers a glimpse into the evolution of temple architecture in India.


Pattadakal: This UNESCO World Heritage Site, roughly 2.5 hours from Hampi, is famous for its group of magnificent temples that showcase a blend of North Indian and South Indian architectural styles.


Anegundi: Just across the Tungabhadra River from Hampi, Anegundi is believed to be the mythical Kishkindha from the Ramayana. You can explore ancient temples, cave paintings, and the charming rural life of this historic village.
It's an ideal spot for a day trip to enjoy the serene surroundings and cool off in the water. Hampi's neighbouring town, Hospet, serves as a gateway to the heritage site.

Hemagiri Falls: Located about an hour's drive from Hampi, these picturesque waterfalls provide a refreshing natural retreat. It's an ideal spot for a day trip to enjoy the serene surroundings and cool off in the water.


Kishkindha Water Park: Located near Anegundi, this water park is a great place for families to relax and enjoy various water attractions.


Sanapur Lake: A scenic lake near Hampi, Sanapur Lake offers opportunities for swimming, picnicking, and simply unwinding by the water's edge.


Malyavanta Raghunatha Temple: Situated on the outskirts of Hampi, this temple is known for its stunning architecture and is a peaceful place to visit.


Talakadu: A bit farther from Hampi (about 5 hours), Talakadu is famous for its enigmatic sand dunes and temples submerged in sand. It's a unique and surreal destination. Located about 5 hours from Hampi, this is the confluence of the Krishna and Malaprabha rivers.

Kudala Sangama: Located about 5 hours from Hampi, this is the confluence of the Krishna and Malaprabha rivers. It's a significant pilgrimage site with a beautiful temple complex.


Kudala Sangama is a sacred pilgrimage site located in the Bagalkot district of the Indian state of Karnataka. It holds great religious significance for Hindus, particularly devotees of Lord Basavanna, a revered 12th-century philosopher and social reformer who played a pivotal role in the Bhakti movement in Karnataka.



Aside from its religious importance, Kudala Sangama is set in a picturesque natural environment, with the lush greenery and the confluence of two rivers providing a tranquil and serene atmosphere.
