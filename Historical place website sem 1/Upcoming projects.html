Kudala Sangama: Located about 5 hours from Hampi, this is the confluence of the Krishna and Malaprabha rivers. It's a significant pilgrimage site with a beautiful temple complex.


Kudala Sangama is a sacred pilgrimage site located in the Bagalkot district of the Indian state of Karnataka. It holds great religious significance for Hindus, particularly devotees of Lord Basavanna, a revered 12th-century philosopher and social reformer who played a pivotal role in the Bhakti movement in Karnataka.



Aside from its religious importance, Kudala Sangama is set in a picturesque natural environment, with the lush greenery and the confluence of two rivers providing a tranquil and serene atmosphere. Sanapur Lake is located 9 km west of Anegundi, Karnataka, off Huligi-Gangavathi road, on the left bank of river Tungabhadra.

Lotus Mahal: Also known as Kamal Mahal, this unique structure is characterised by its lotus-shaped arches and was likely a palace for the royal women.


It showcases a beautiful blend of Indo-Islamic architectural styles, with elements from both Hindu and Islamic architecture. The structure is two-storied and made primarily of brick and mortar, with ornate stucco work and carvings adorning its exteriors.
